Pumpkin Pasta Sauce With Bootstrap

This Pumpkin Pasta Sauce recipe is one of my favourite recipies to make in the fall and it is a recipe I found off of the internet but changed so much of it, it's like a whole new recipe. The sauce used for the recipe is a perfect mix of sweet and salty for a nice Pasta Sauce. This pasta sauce was originallly an idea from my brother but because I am the cook of the house he asked me to help him with the recipe but I ended up doing all of the work.

Ingredients

  • 2 cloves of garlic
  • 1 Tbsp butter
  • 1 cup of pumpkin puree
  • 1/8 tsp nutmeg
  • 1/4 tsp chili powder
  • A pinch of pepper
  • a very small dash of pumpkin spice seasoning
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 1/4 cup of parmesean cheese
  • 3 tbsp heavy cream
  • 8oz pasta of your choice
  • 1 cup chicken broth

Directions

  1. Begin boiling a large pot of water for your pasta.
    Once the water is boiling, add the pasta and boil until al dente (7-10 minutes). Drain in a colander.
  2. and add them to a large skillet with one tablespoon of butter.
    Sauté over medium-low heat for 1-2 minutes, or just until the garlic is soft and fragrant.
  3. Add the pumpkin purée and chicken broth to the skillet and stir to combine. Add the nutmeg, chili powder, cayenne pepper, and some freshly cracked black pepper.
    Stir in the spices and let the sauce simmer over medium-low heat while the pasta cooks (about 10 minutes), stirring occasionally.
  4. Once the pasta is finished cooking and is draining in a colander, add the half and half or cream to the skillet and stir it into the pumpkin sauce.
    Taste the sauce and add salt if needed (this will depend on the type of broth you use). Finally, stir the drained pasta in to the sauce and serve.
